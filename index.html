The History of Olympics

Since 1984 The Olympics began in ancient Greece in 776 BCE as part of religious festivals honoring Zeus. They featured atheletic competitions among city-states and were held every four years. The Games were abolished in 393 CE by the Roman Empire.

In 1896, Pierre de Coubertin revived them as the present day Olympics, promoting international cohesion and sportsmanship. The Olympics have considering the fact that developed into a global occasion, symbolizing peace and excellence.

Olympics unites athletes from around the world, showcasing different cultures and backgrounds. It promotes respect and friendship, bringing people together through sport.
